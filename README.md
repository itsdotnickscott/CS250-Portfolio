# CS250-Portfolio

## How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
User needs can usually be explained as a feature or an action that can be taken within the app. This is why user stories are helpful - they give a picture of what the user is trying to do, and for what purpose. Not only do user stories give developers insight into what feature should be developed, but also the priority for it within the project as a whole. Agile's priority of keeping the clients and customers in mind co-align with creating user stories and creating a "user-first" design.

## How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach programs with the user-experience first. This means that design choices should be made so that it is easy to understand and use. I like the Sprint-style work of an Agile process and hope to incorporate it in further development work. Constant iteration means that any failures or roadblocks can be moved past quickly. I like the prioritization of tasks into a product backlog and choosing which stories to implement for each iterative sprint.

## What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member is being open in communication and honest. It is okay to have concerns or setbacks, which is why the daily Scrum meetings are important for a team to understand and address. Being a good team member supporting others when they need the help. Being honest and constructive is an important skill to fit in the mold of a team, as being overly critical can hinder progress. Being a good team member means meeting deadlines to keep the flow of the team, and knowing what resources to go to to meet those deadlines and create a quality product.
